Debuting in Amazing Fantasy #15 (1962), Spider-Man is one of Marvel Comics most iconic and long-running superhero series. Created by Stan Lee and Steve Ditko, the character follows Peter Parker, a teenager who gains spider-like abilities after a radioactive spider bite. Core comic lines include The Amazing Spider-Man, Spectacular Spider-Man, Ultimate Spider-Man, and numerous reboots and alternate-universe stories. Across decades, Spider-Mans narrative blends superhero action with grounded personal drama—balancing school, work, relationships, and the responsibility that comes with power. Key themes include responsibility, sacrifice, identity, and perseverance. Major villains such as Green Goblin, Doctor Octopus, Venom, and Kingpin help define the characters legacy. Spider-Man comics are known for their accessibility, emotional storytelling, and long-running continuity that allows readers to grow alongside Peter Parker.
