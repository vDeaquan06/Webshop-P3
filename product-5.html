First appearing in Fantastic Four #52 (1966), Black Panther was created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby and became Marvels first mainstream Black superhero. The comics follow TChalla, king of Wakanda—a technologically advanced African nation rich in the rare metal vibranium. Over decades, multiple writers have expanded the character through solo series and team appearances in Avengers. Notable runs include Christopher Priests political reinterpretation, Reginald Hudlins modernization of Wakanda, and Ta-Nehisi Coates acclaimed exploration of monarchy, revolution, and identity.
